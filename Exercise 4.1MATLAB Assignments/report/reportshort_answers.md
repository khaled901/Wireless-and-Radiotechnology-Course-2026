# Short Answers (Lecture-2)

## Q1
Time domain shows how a signal changes with time, so we can see its waveform shape.
Frequency domain shows which frequencies exist inside the signal and how strong each one is.
Using frequency domain helps us understand signal components clearly, especially in RF systems.

## Q2
Filtering helps RF receivers by selecting the desired signal frequency and rejecting noise and interference.
It improves signal quality and allows the receiver to focus on the useful information only.

## Q3
Modulation moves a low-frequency baseband signal to a higher carrier frequency.
This makes the signal suitable for wireless transmission and allows multiple signals to share the spectrum.

## Q4
The easiest filter to design was the low-pass filter.
It only needs one cutoff frequency and clearly separates low frequencies from higher ones.

## Q5
After modulation, new frequency components appear around the carrier frequency.
They are located at fc − fm and fc + fm (sidebands).
